Restless Multi-Armed Bandits

Scalable resource allocation algorithms for health, conservation, and beyond.

Restless Multi-Armed Bandits (RMABs) model the problem of allocating scarce resources (interventions, attention, budget) across many agents whose states evolve over time. We develop scalable algorithms and deploy them in real-world systems.

Key directions:

  • Whittle index policies and online learning for unknown dynamics
  • Decision-focused learning of RMAB transition parameters
  • Neural index policies for multi-action, heterogeneous-budget settings
  • Non-stationary and networked RMAB extensions

Deployments:

  • Maternal and child health outreach scheduling (ARMMAN, India)
  • Pediatric early warning systems (Children’s Healthcare of Atlanta)
  • Green security patrol scheduling (wildlife conservation)

Selected publications:

(Wang et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023; Verma et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2021; Pandey et al., 2025)
